Our Product
Enview allows clients to understand their energy infrastructure in ways not previously possible. We extract insights by automating the analysis of multiple data sources to detect and predict issues before they become threats that affect millions of families and businesses. Our clients trust us to increase the safety and reliability of their transmission powerlines and oil & gas pipelines.

Our Team
Enview’s team has deep experience in applying advanced data analytics to solve impactful, real-world issues. We are passionate about using our expertise in computer vision, machine learning, and data visualization to solve challenging problems that matter.
San Gunawardana
Co-Founder & CEO
After finishing a PhD in aerospace engineering at Stanford, San went to Afghanistan where he developed data analytics and remote sensing to predict threats and prevent incidents. San is excited to apply those insights to help the energy sector solve impactful problems. San has done computer vision at NASA, built imaging satellites with the Air Force, and was an early employee at ICON Aircraft.

Lead Front-End Engineer
  • Location:
  • San Fransisco
  • Salary:
  • $105k - $135k + Equity
Mission of Role:

Front-end development of web sites and apps that use web mapping and data visualization to tell impactful stories based on aerial imagery.

We Are:

Enview creates data analytics to protect the world’s energy infrastructure. We extract novel and actionable insights by automating the fusion and analysis of multiple data sources (multispectral aerial & satellite imagery, LiDAR, GIS, etc.) to detect and predict issues before they become threats that affect millions of families and businesses.

The Challenge:

Are you excited by the idea of delivering insights that materially impact the safety and well-being of millions of people? Our intelligent algorithms scientists are doing amazing things to generate complex, data-driven insights, but those insights aren’t worth a thing unless we can effectively present them to people. That’s where you come in—help us generate and tell amazing stories using beautiful interactive web applications.

You Will:
  • Design & architect a modern web app from the ground up. You have responsibility for choosing the framework that you think is most appropriate (whether that’s Node, Rails, Meteor, Sinatra, or something else)
  • Develop & implement web maps to present geospatial info to clients
  • Have an appreciation and ability to implement elegant and intuitive user interfaces
  • Develop & implement interactive tools for users to annotate aerial images
  • Create a tool that automatically overlays data visualization & visual design elements onto aerial imagery
  • Build a web front-end for interaction with a geospatial database
  • Actually walk-the-talk on software design best-practices, including (but not limited to!) test-driven development and frequent end-to-end testing
Strong Candidates Will Have:
  • Experience with web mapping tools such as: OpenLayers, Leaflet, OpenStreetMap, Mapbox, turf.js, etc.
  • Familiarity with data visualization tools such as: D3, C3, WebGL, etc.
  • Experience developing in Python
Perks:
  • Opportunity to play a foundational role as one of the first five employees at a well-funded startup
  • Work with people like yourself—talented and thoughtful; passionate about solving challenging problems; and who do awesome things outside of work
  • Unlimited vacation policy (we’re serious about this—do great work, have fun and live life)
  • Medical/vision/dental
To Apply:

Email careers@enview.com with a resume. Links to portfolios, GitHub, etc. are always appreciated.

Computer Vision Engineer
  • Location:
  • San Fransico
  • Salary:
  • $115k - $135k + Equity
Mission of Role:

Develop computer vision analyses using aerial imagery to identify and classify potential threats and impacts to energy infrastructure.

We Are:

Enview creates data analytics to protect the world’s energy infrastructure. We extract novel and actionable insights by automating the fusion and analysis of multiple data sources (multispectral aerial & satellite imagery, LiDAR, GIS, etc.) to detect and predict issues before they become threats that affect millions of families and businesses.

The Challenge:

We have a firehose of aerospace imagery that spans the spectrum from visual to long-wave IR (and we’re already thinking about UV). We’re going to identify and classify everything from human actions (oil spills, gas explosions, dangerous construction) to natural forces (landslides, flooding, forest fires). We’re excited to use CV to understand our world better than ever, and we hope you are too.

You Will:
  • Develop change detection algorithms on aerial imagery (visual & IR) to identify rapidly emerging threats and gradual environmental changes
  • Develop algorithms to identify/classify potential threats and normal activities
  • Develop automated process to georegister aerial/satellite imagery
  • Actually walk-the-talk on software design best-practices, including (but not limited to!) test-driven development and frequent end-to-end testing
Strong Candidates Will Have:
  • MS or PhD in engineering or computer science
  • Experience in one or more of the following image processing topics: feature extraction and matching, bundle adjustment, classification, etc.
  • Experience to know where all your favorite techniques fail and how to compensate for that
  • Software prototyping and implementation skills in Python or C/C++
  • Conversant with machine learning techniques a strong plus
  • Experience in web-mapping or GIS analysis a plus
